The Fat, the Poor and the Unhappy

The body weight of the population in a country might have different relationships with prosperity of a country, commonly measured in terms of its annual per capita Gross Domestic Product (GDP), and happiness of a population. On this webpage the relationship between the body weight of a population and the level of income and happiness is examined. In the worldmap the BMI per country and the percentages of overweight (BMI ≥ 25) and obesity (BMI ≥ 30) are displayed. You can hover over a country in the worldmap to see the level of income and happiness relative to other countries both in the barchart and scatterplot. You can also look for a specific country in the table and sort the table from lowest to highest for all the different categories by clicking on the category in the header of the table.
